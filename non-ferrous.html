Non-Ferrous Metal Scrap

Non-ferrous metal scrap plays a vital role in the global recycling industry, as these metals retain their properties even after multiple recycling cycles, making them highly sustainable. The recycling process involves collecting and processing scrap from various sources, such as manufacturing waste, demolished structures, and end-of-life products. By reintegrating these metals into the production cycle, industries can reduce their dependence on mining and processing raw materials, which in turn decreases environmental degradation and resource depletion. Additionally, the economic value of non-ferrous scrap is significant, as it helps lower production costs and drives the circular economy, promoting a more responsible approach to resource management.

1)Magnesium Scrap & Aluminium Tense Scrap

Magnesium Scrap : Magnesium is highly valued for its low density and high strength, making it useful in aerospace, automotive, and electronics industries. Recycling magnesium scrap helps conserve resources and reduces the environmental impact associated with magnesium extraction and processing. The recycling process involves collecting, sorting, and melting the scrap to create new magnesium products, ensuring efficient use of this valuable metal.

Aluminium Tense Scrap: This category includes heavier scrap metal that may have some coatings or contaminants, but still consists mainly of iron and steel. HSM 2 generally has lower quality compared to HSM 1 and may require additional processing before being used in steelmaking.

2)UBC(Used Beverage Can) (Taldon) Scrap

UBC scrap, commonly referred to as Taldon scrap, consists of aluminum beverage cans that are recycled to recover aluminum. These cans are a significant source of recycled aluminum, which is highly valued for its sustainability and energy efficiency. Key points include:

Source: UBC scrap is typically collected from discarded beverage cans, which are made from aluminum.


Processing: The scrap is cleaned, shredded, and melted down to produce new aluminum products. This process helps reduce the need for raw aluminum extraction and conserves energy.


Benefits: Recycling UBC scrap conserves natural resources, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and minimizes waste in landfills.

3)Aluminium 6063 Extrusion Scrap

Aluminium 6063 extrusion scrap consists of waste materials from the production or use of aluminum products made from the 6063 alloy. This alloy is known for its excellent corrosion resistance, medium strength, and good surface finish, making it ideal for extrusion into various shapes such as:

Window Frames: Commonly used in architectural applications.


Ductile Iron Pipes: These pipes are a modern variant of cast iron pipes, featuring improved tensile strength and ductility. Ductile iron pipes are used in water supply and wastewater systems due to their enhanced flexibility and resistance to impact.


Door Frames: Lightweight and durable, ideal for building projects.


Tubing: Used in transportation, automotive, and construction industries.

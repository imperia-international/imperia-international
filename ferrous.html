Ferrous Metal Scrap

Ferrous metal scrap consists of discarded materials primarily made of iron or iron alloys. Characterized by their magnetic properties due to high iron content, these metals, such as steel and cast iron, are renowned for their strength and durability.

Recycling ferrous metal scrap is crucial for sustainable manufacturing and resource conservation. Instead of extracting raw iron, recycling helps to conserve natural resources and lessen the environmental impact of mining and production. The recycling process involves collecting, sorting, processing, and melting the scrap to produce new materials and products.

1)HSM 1&2

HSM 1 (Heavy Melting Scrap 1): This category includes clean, uncoated scrap metal that is primarily composed of iron and steel. HSM 1 is typically free from contaminants like insulation, paint, or other coatings. It is suitable for direct melting and recycling in steel production.

HSM 2 (Heavy Melting Scrap 2): This category includes heavier scrap metal that may have some coatings or contaminants, but still consists mainly of iron and steel. HSM 2 generally has lower quality compared to HSM 1 and may require additional processing before being used in steelmaking.

2)LMS(Light Melting Scrap)

LMS consists of lighter metal scrap, often including materials like thin gauge sheet metal, small metal parts, and other non-heavy metal scrap. Unlike Heavy Melting Scrap (HMS), LMS may contain more contaminants or coatings but still predominantly consists of iron or steel.

This type of scrap is typically processed in steelmaking facilities to be melted down and recycled. Due to its lighter nature, LMS often requires different handling and processing compared to heavier scrap materials.

3)Iron Pipes

Cast Iron Pipes: Known for their durability and strength, cast iron pipes are commonly used in sewer and drainage systems. They are resistant to fire and can handle high pressure, making them ideal for heavy-duty applications.

Ductile Iron Pipes: These pipes are a modern variant of cast iron pipes, featuring improved tensile strength and ductility. Ductile iron pipes are used in water supply and wastewater systems due to their enhanced flexibility and resistance to impact.


Malleable Iron Pipes: Used in fittings and small diameter pipes, malleable iron pipes are known for their ability to be shaped and their good tensile strength. They are used in plumbing and industrial applications.
